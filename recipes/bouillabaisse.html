Bouillabaisse

Description

Bouillabaisse is a traditional Provencal fish soup originating in the port city Marseille. Traditionally it is made with 3 fish and other seafood but newer itterations of it have seen exclusions of the 3 fish rule and in some cases seafood altogether. Vegetables such as onions, celery, tomatoes, fennel and potatoes are simmered in the broth and served along side the fish. The broth is typically served seperate from the fish with bread and rouille, a mayonaisse of olive oil, garlic, saffron, and cayenne pepper. The rouille may also contain reduced fish stock in newer iterations.

Ingredients:

    >For the Broth:

  • 1/2 cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 large yellow onion, diced
  • 1 large leek, washed of any grit and diced
  • 1 medium fennel bulb, cored and diced
  • 5 medium cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1/2 teaspoon whole fennel seeds
  • 2 large pinches saffron threads
  • One (2-inch) strip zest from 1 orange
  • Large pinch cayenne pepper or other red chile powder
  • 2 sprigs thyme
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 6 plum tomatoes, cored, seeded, and diced
  • 2 pounds whole fish or fish bones and heads
  • 2 cups dry white wine
  • 1/4 cup Pernod
  • 2 quarts boiling water
  • 2 sprigs flat-leafed parsley
  • 1 bayleaf
